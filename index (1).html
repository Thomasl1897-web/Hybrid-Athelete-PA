System Rules

Progression Guidelines

How to advance safely across the 6–8 week cycle

Double Progression Model

  • Each exercise has a rep range (e.g., 8–10). Stay at the same weight until you hit the top of the range for all working sets at the prescribed RIR.
  • Once you do, increase the load next session — jump by the smallest available dumbbell increment (typically 2–4kg per side). Rep count will likely drop back toward the bottom of the range; that's expected and correct.

RIR Targets by Movement Type

RIR 3–4
Power / explosive lifts — snatch, swings, jump squats, thrusters. Never train these to failure; speed and crispness matter more than fatigue.
RIR 1–2 final set
Heavy compound hypertrophy lifts — bench, squat, RDL, rows. RIR 2–3 on earlier sets.
RIR 2–3
Accessory / unilateral work — enough stimulus without excess joint stress.
RIR 3+
Prehab work — rotator cuff, tibialis, Y-T-W. Always — this is about quality motor patterning, not overload.
Near failure OK
Core / bracing — can be taken close to failure since these are low systemic fatigue.

Managing Fatigue

  • Track session-to-session: if your top-set weight or reps stalls or drops for 2 consecutive sessions on a given lift, that's your first fatigue flag — not a reason to push harder, a reason to check sleep, nutrition, and stress.
  • Joint discomfort (as opposed to muscular fatigue) on any rep = stop that exercise for the day. Don't push through it.

Deload Protocol

Every 5th–6th week, or whenever 2+ fatigue flags appear:

  • Cut working-set volume by ~40–50% (e.g., 4 sets → 2 sets) or reduce load by 15–20% while keeping normal rep ranges.
  • Drop power/explosive work intensity too — treat it as a technique-refinement week, not a taper for a max effort.
  • Keep frequency the same; this is a volume/intensity deload, not a rest week. Movement quality and joint-health work should stay untouched or even increase slightly.

Advancing the Whole Program

After 6–8 weeks, choose one:

  • Increase overall volume — add a set to key lifts — if you're recovering well, or
  • Tighten rep ranges downward (e.g., shift 8–10 → 6–8) for a strength-emphasis block, then cycle back to hypertrophy ranges after.
